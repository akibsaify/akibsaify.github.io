Most people on Amazon India pay more than they need to. Not because the deals aren't there — but because they don't know how to stack discounts.

On any given product, you might be able to combine a coupon, a bank card offer, Amazon Pay cashback, and an exchange bonus. Together, these can knock 10-20% off a price that's already discounted.

Here's how each one works and how to use them together.

## 1. Amazon Coupons (The Ones Most People Miss)

On many product pages, right below the price, there's a small checkbox that says something like "Apply ₹200 coupon" or "Save 5% with coupon." It's easy to miss because Amazon doesn't make it obvious.

**How to use it:** Just click the checkbox before adding to cart. The discount applies automatically at checkout.

**Where to find all available coupons:** Go to amazon.in/coupons — this page lists all active coupons across categories. You can clip them in advance and they'll apply automatically when you buy an eligible product.

**Pro tip:** Some coupons are "clip and save" — you need to click them before they work. Others auto-apply. Always check the product page.

## 2. Bank Card Offers

During sales and sometimes even on regular days, Amazon partners with specific banks to offer instant discounts. These typically work like this:

- **10% instant discount up to ₹1,500** with SBI credit card
- **5% cashback** with Amazon Pay ICICI credit card
- **₹2,000 off on orders above ₹15,000** with HDFC debit card

**How to use it:** Just pay with the specified card. The discount applies automatically at checkout.

**Which card is best for Amazon?** The Amazon Pay ICICI credit card gives you 5% back on every Prime purchase, all year round — not just during sales. If you buy regularly from Amazon, this single card can save you ₹5,000-10,000 a year.

**Important:** Bank offers usually have a maximum discount cap. "10% off up to ₹1,500" means on a ₹20,000 purchase, you'd get ₹1,500 off — not ₹2,000. Read the terms.

## 3. Amazon Pay Balance Cashback

Loading money into your Amazon Pay wallet sometimes earns you cashback. Amazon runs these promotions regularly:

- "Add ₹1,000, get ₹50 cashback"
- "First load bonus: Add ₹500, get ₹75 back"

**How to use it:** Go to Amazon Pay > Add Balance > Check for offers before loading.

**Pro tip:** Load your balance right before a big sale. Cashback offers are more generous during Amazon Great Indian Festival and Prime Day.

## 4. Exchange Offers

For phones, laptops, TVs, and appliances, Amazon offers exchange discounts when you trade in your old device. These can be surprisingly generous:

- Old phone (even a basic one): ₹1,000-5,000 off
- Old laptop: ₹2,000-8,000 off
- Old TV: ₹3,000-10,000 off
- Old appliance: ₹1,000-5,000 off

**How to use it:** Look for the "with exchange" option on the product page. You'll need to specify what you're trading in, and Amazon will estimate a value.

**Pro tip:** The exchange value for your old device varies day by day. Check back a few times before committing — you might get a better value closer to a sale.

## 5. No Cost EMI

This isn't exactly a discount, but it saves you real money. "No Cost EMI" means you pay in installments without any interest — the interest is subsidised by the brand or Amazon.

For expensive purchases (phones, laptops, appliances), this is genuinely useful. Instead of paying ₹40,000 at once, you pay ₹6,667 per month for 6 months, with zero interest.

**How to check eligibility:** Look for the "EMI options" section on any product page. Not all products qualify, and the available tenures vary.

## How to Stack Everything Together

Here's an example of how stacking works on a real purchase:

**Product:** A smartphone with MRP ₹18,999

| Discount Type | Saving |
|---|---|
| Sale price (30% off) | ₹13,299 |
| Amazon coupon (₹500 off) | ₹12,799 |
| SBI card offer (10% up to ₹1,000) | ₹11,799 |
| Exchange old phone | ₹9,799 (₹2,000 exchange value) |
| **Total paid** | **₹9,799** |
| **Total saved** | **₹9,200 (48% off MRP)** |

That's ₹9,200 saved by stacking four different discounts — on top of the sale price.

## Quick Checklist Before Every Purchase

Before clicking "Buy Now" on any Amazon purchase, run through this list:

1. Is there a coupon on the product page? Clip it.
2. Is there a bank card offer? Use the right card.
3. Is there an exchange offer? Trade in your old device.
4. Is No Cost EMI available? Consider it for big purchases.
5. Check amazon.in/coupons for any category-wide coupons.
6. Check if loading Amazon Pay balance has any cashback offer.

It takes 2 minutes and can save you hundreds to thousands of rupees on every order.
